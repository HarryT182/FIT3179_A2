Melbourne Metro Stations

The Train Network

This map shows all 222 Metro railway stations in Melbourne. It shows the total number of station entries that occured during the 23-24 Financial Year.

Each square is a station. Hover over a station for details, including the average entries per normal weekday and school holiday.

Flinders Street Station was the most used, followed by Southern Cross, Melbourne Central and Parliment of the City Loop.

Major Interchange Stations such as Richmond and Footscray are also among the highest.

Grey lines are railway tracks. Many tracks are not in use.

Station Entries by Suburb

This map shows the total number of station entries by suburb - i.e., the number of entries of any station within that suburb. Hover over a suburb to view.

Many suburbs have no stations. These are shown in grey.

Unsurprisingly, Melbourne and Docklands, comprising the City Loop, have the most number of entries.

Use of the City Loop

This bar chart shows the average entries per day for stations on the City Loop.

It is divided between weekdays and school holiday weekdays.. Weekends are not shown.

Overall, people use these stations slightly less during school holidays.
